Traditional Synthetic Fabrics: The Old Standard

For years, synthetic fabrics like polyamide (nylon) and polyester have dominated performance wear. While offering durability, they come with significant drawbacks that impact user comfort and health.

Studies have indicated that polyamide fabrics, in particular, may contain elevated levels of Bisphenol A (BPA). BPA is a chemical compound linked to various serious health risks, including certain types of cancer and hormonal disruptions. This poses a significant concern for prolonged skin contact, especially in active wear.

A primary limitation of traditional synthetic fabrics is their inability to efficiently manage moisture. While they can absorb sweat from the skin, they often lack the crucial ability to transfer that moisture to the fabric's surface for rapid evaporation. This leads to:

  • Retained Dampness: The fabric remains wet, leaving both the garment and the skin feeling damp.
  • Discomfort: A persistent wet sensation causes significant discomfort during wear.
  • Increased Body Temperature: Poor evaporation hinders the body's natural cooling process, leading to a rise in body temperature.
  • Heavy Garment Feel: As the fabric retains moisture, it feels increasingly heavy, hindering performance and comfort.

Conbello Textile's Innovation: Air-Layer Technology

Conbello Textile's R&D department has engineered a revolutionary fabric to overcome these challenges, setting a new benchmark for textile performance.

Our next-generation technical fabric incorporates advanced air-layer technology. This innovative construction introduces a microfilm-like air gap strategically positioned between the skin and the textile. This unique feature is fundamental to its superior performance.

The integrated air gap results in a substantial reduction in direct skin contact and friction. This critical design element directly contributes to:

  • Minimized Sweating: Less contact means less heat trapped against the skin, naturally reducing sweat production.
  • Lowered Irritation Risk: Reduced friction significantly minimizes the likelihood of skin chafing, rashes, and general irritation, especially during prolonged wear or high activity.
  • Reduced Allergic Reactions: By creating a barrier and minimizing direct chemical exposure from the fabric, the risk of allergic reactions is substantially lowered, making it ideal for sensitive skin.
